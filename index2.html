Round 1 of 4
The Scope Bomb
📱 Slack — from: Marco (Senior Dev) · 9:02 AM
"Hey, welcome aboard. Quick heads-up — the client added a live chat feature to the requirements last week. Nobody told the old PM officially, but it's in the latest email chain. It's... not small."
Situation
You dig into the project files. The live chat feature would take at least 2 weeks to build — and you only have 14 days until launch. The original approved scope doesn't include it. The client's email does. Your team doesn't know yet.

This is your first decision as PM.

Team vs. Client

"The live chat is non-negotiable for us — our student board voted on it. I trust your team can make it work. We're counting on you."
Situation
Your lead developer pulls you aside: "If we build live chat, we skip testing. And if we skip testing, I can't promise the app won't crash on launch day — in front of everyone."

Director Santos has no technical background. She believes "make it work" is a reasonable ask. Your team is already logging 10-hour days.

Round 3 of 4
The Cover-Up Temptation
💬 DM — from: Rina (UX Lead) · 3:15 PM
"I found a bug in the event registration flow. It's not crashing the app — but it records duplicate signups sometimes. Maybe 1 in 10 users. I don't think anyone else noticed."
Situation
You're 4 days from launch. Fixing this bug properly takes 2–3 days. A quick patch might reduce it to 1 in 20 users. Or you could log it as a known issue, launch, and fix it post-launch — but the client won't know. This is an ethics decision.

Launch or Hold?
📱 Text — from: Director Santos · 6:47 AM (Launch Day)
"Good morning! We're so excited. 300 students registered overnight. The dean's office will be watching the dashboard live. Today's the day! 🎉"
Situation
It's launch morning. Your developer just found that under heavy load — like, say, 300 concurrent signups — the server may time out. It's never been tested at this scale. It might be fine. It might not.

The dean is watching. 300 students are waiting. You have 45 minutes before the platform goes live.
